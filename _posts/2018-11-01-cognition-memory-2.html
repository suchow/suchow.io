Manipulating the timing and other factors affect perception of causality. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark From Heider & Simmel's movies of correlated movements of shape, we infer a whole world of causal factors, such as chasing and hiding. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark Causal graphical models (Bayes nets) are a useful tool for representing the causal structure of probabilistic events. They are made up of nodes that represent events or properties, directed edges that represent causation, and (conditional) probability tables that attach to nodes. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark One can draw a casual model that reflects our best understanding of causality, or instead one that reflects a person's incorrect theory about how a particular causal system works. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark Occam's razor is the idea that simpler explanations are better explanations. But what is "simpler"? Fewer root causes? Fewer nodes in the casual graphical model? It's up for debate. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark Networks aren't useful only for representing causality through Bayes nets. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark We can use networks to represent the structure of our knowledge. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark We can use networks to describe social ties. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark We can use networks to learn. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark Networks are made up of {nodes, vertices, entities} and {edges, links}, and can be directed or undirected, weighted or unweighted. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark We can also represent other people, their thoughts, their thoughts about our thoughts, &c. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark Theory of mind is the spark of social cognition, as seen in the Sally-Anne task. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark There is a debate as to whether theory of mind is best thought of as an intuitive theory ("Theory theory") or rather is the result of simulating what others would think using your own mind ("Simulation theory"). --- class: center, middle, statement, dark Many social scenarios benefit from social reasoning and their essences are captured in economic games. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark Consider Keynesian beauty contests. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark Schelling coordination games and focal points ("Schelling points"). --- class: center, middle, statement, dark And the El Farol Problem / minority game. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark But social reasoning has its limits — it is bounded. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark One such limit is a recursion limit, how many nested layers of thinking about think we can do. --- class: center, middle, statement, dark Vision is a process of inference. War of the Ghosts One night two young men from Egulac went down to the river to hunt seals and while they were there it became foggy and calm. Then they heard war-cries, and they thought: "Maybe this is a war-party". They escaped to the shore, and hid behind a log. Now canoes came up, and they heard the noise of paddles, and saw one canoe coming up to them. There were five men in the canoe, and they said: "What do you think? We wish to take you along. We are going up the river to make war on the people." One of the young men said,"I have no arrows." "Arrows are in the canoe," they said. "I will not go along. I might be killed. My relatives do not know where I have gone. But you," he said, turning to the other, "may go with them." So one of the young men went, but the other returned home. And the warriors went on up the river to a town on the other side of Kalama. The people came down to the water and they began to fight, and many were killed. But presently the young man heard one of the warriors say, "Quick, let us go home: that Indian has been hit." Now he thought: "Oh, they are ghosts." He did not feel sick, but they said he had been shot. So the canoes went back to Egulac and the young man went ashore to his house and made a fire. And he told everybody and said: "Behold I accompanied the ghosts, and we went to fight. Many of our fellows were killed, and many of those who attacked us were killed. They said I was hit, and I did not feel sick." He told it all, and then he became quiet. When the sun rose he fell down. Something black came out of his mouth. His face became contorted. The people jumped up and cried. He was dead. --- # The misinformation effect --- # DRM paradigm "Deese-Roediger-McDermott" - Free recall - Random vs. semantically related - Intrusions, false memories - Assignment 8. --- class: center, middle # Fin.
