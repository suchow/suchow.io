Two members of a criminal gang are arrested and imprisoned. Each prisoner is in solitary confinement with no means of communicating with the other. The prosecutors lack sufficient evidence to convict the pair on the principal charge, but they have enough to convict both on a lesser charge. Simultaneously, the prosecutors offer each prisoner a bargain. Each prisoner is given the opportunity either to betray the other by testifying that the other committed the crime, or to cooperate with the other by remaining silent. The possible outcomes are: If A and B each betray the other, each of them serves two years in prison If A betrays B but B remains silent, A will be set free and B will serve three years in prison If A remains silent but B betrays A, A will serve three years in prison and B will be set free If A and B both remain silent, both of them will serve only one year in prison (on the lesser charge). --- # Pareto optimality, Nash equilbrium - Pareto: nobody can switch without making other worse off - Nash: strategies are best responses to the others' --- # One shot vs. repeated games - "Iterated" prisoner's dilemma - Chance to learn, use feedback - What changes? --- # Strategies in the IDP - All cooperate - All defect - Random and... --- # Tit-for-tat - Robert Axelrod's tournaments - Nice and forgiving --- # Evolutionary IDP - What is the object of evolution? - What is the "genome" of cooperation? - What is selection? --- class: center, middle # Fin.
